Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)

The Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) is an entry requirement for visa-exempt foreign nationals who are traveling to Canada by air. Introduced in 2015, the eTA is electronically linked to the traveler’s passport and is valid for up to five years or until the passport expires, whichever comes first. The eTA allows travelers to fly to Canada for short stays, usually for tourism, business, or transit purposes, without needing a traditional visa. However, travelers must meet certain conditions to be eligible for an eTA, and they should obtain it before boarding their flight to Canada.

The eTA is part of Canada’s efforts to enhance security and streamline the entry process for low-risk travelers. It allows the government to pre-screen travelers for admissibility before they arrive in Canada, reducing the risk of inadmissible individuals entering the country. The eTA application process is straightforward and typically takes only a few minutes to complete online, making it a convenient option for eligible travelers.

Key Features of the Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)

The eTA offers several key features that make it a convenient and efficient entry requirement for eligible travelers:

  • Eligibility for Visa-Exempt Travelers: The eTA is required for travelers from visa-exempt countries who are flying to Canada. It is not required for U.S. citizens, but U.S. permanent residents need an eTA.
  • Electronic and Easy Application Process: The eTA application is completed online, and most applications are approved within minutes. The process involves filling out a simple form and providing basic personal information.
  • Validity Period: An eTA is valid for up to five years or until the passport expires, whichever comes first. It allows for multiple entries to Canada during its validity period.
  • Linked to Passport: The eTA is electronically linked to the traveler’s passport, eliminating the need for a physical document. Travelers must present the same passport used for the eTA application when boarding their flight to Canada.
  • Short Stay Authorization: An eTA allows travelers to stay in Canada for up to six months per visit. The exact length of stay is determined by a Canadian border services officer upon arrival.
  • Pre-Screening for Admissibility: The eTA allows Canadian authorities to pre-screen travelers for security, criminal, and health risks before they arrive in Canada, enhancing border security.
Who Needs an eTA?

Not all travelers need an eTA to fly to Canada. Here is a breakdown of who requires an eTA:

  • Visa-Exempt Foreign Nationals: Citizens of countries that are exempt from obtaining a visa to visit Canada (such as the United Kingdom, Australia, Japan, and many European Union countries) must obtain an eTA if they are traveling to Canada by air.
  • Permanent Residents of the United States: U.S. Green Card holders need an eTA to fly to or transit through Canada. They must also carry a valid passport and their U.S. Green Card when traveling to Canada.
  • Individuals Traveling by Air: The eTA requirement applies only to those entering Canada by air. It is not required for entry by land or sea (such as by car, bus, train, or cruise ship).
Who Does Not Need an eTA?

The following travelers do not require an eTA to enter Canada:

  • U.S. Citizens: U.S. citizens do not need an eTA or a visa to enter Canada. They must carry a valid U.S. passport when flying to Canada.
  • Canadian Citizens and Dual Citizens: Canadian citizens, including dual citizens, cannot apply for an eTA. They must travel with a valid Canadian passport.
  • Permanent Residents of Canada: Permanent residents of Canada (PR card holders) do not need an eTA. They must carry their PR card or a valid permanent resident travel document (PRTD) when flying to Canada.
  • Travelers Entering by Land or Sea: Travelers entering Canada by land or sea (e.g., by car, bus, train, or cruise ship) do not require an eTA, regardless of their nationality.
Eligibility Requirements for an eTA

To be eligible for an eTA, travelers must meet the following requirements:

  • Valid Passport: Travelers must have a valid passport from a visa-exempt country. The eTA will be electronically linked to this passport.
  • No Criminal Record: Travelers must not have a criminal record that would make them inadmissible to Canada. This includes serious criminal offenses, such as violent crimes or crimes involving moral turpitude.
  • No Security Concerns: Travelers must not pose a security threat to Canada. Individuals with known links to terrorist organizations or other security concerns may be denied an eTA.
  • Good Health: Travelers must be in good health and not have a medical condition that could pose a public health risk to Canada.
  • Intent to Leave Canada: Travelers must demonstrate that they intend to leave Canada at the end of their authorized stay, typically by providing proof of onward travel or ties to their home country.
Application Process for an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)

The application process for an eTA is straightforward and involves several steps. Most eTA applications are approved within minutes, but some may take several days if additional documentation is required. Here is a detailed breakdown of the process:

Step 1: Gather Required Information

Before starting the eTA application, travelers should ensure they have the following information and documents ready:

  • Valid Passport: The passport must be valid for the entire duration of the stay in Canada and come from a visa-exempt country.
  • Credit or Debit Card: A valid credit or debit card is needed to pay the eTA application fee (currently CAD 7).
  • Contact Information: The applicant’s email address will be used for communication and sending the eTA approval.
  • Personal Information: Basic personal details such as name, date of birth, nationality, and passport details.
Step 2: Complete the Online Application

Travelers must complete the eTA application form online through the official Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) website. The form requires personal information, passport details, travel plans, and answers to security and health-related questions. The form should be filled out accurately and truthfully to avoid delays or refusals.

Step 3: Pay the Application Fee

Once the application form is completed, the traveler must pay the eTA application fee using a valid credit or debit card. The fee is currently CAD 7, and payment must be made at the time of application submission. The application will not be processed until the fee is paid.

Step 4: Wait for Approval

After submitting the application and payment, the traveler will receive an email confirming that the application has been received. Most eTA applications are processed and approved within minutes. However, some applications may take several days if additional documentation or information is required. Travelers should check their email regularly for updates.

Step 5: Receive the eTA Approval

If the eTA application is approved, the traveler will receive an email notification with the eTA confirmation. The eTA is electronically linked to the traveler’s passport and does not require a physical document. Travelers should print the confirmation email for their records, although it is not mandatory to present it upon arrival in Canada.

Step 6: Travel to Canada

When traveling to Canada, the traveler must present the same passport used to apply for the eTA. The airline staff will check the passport to ensure it is linked to a valid eTA before boarding the flight to Canada. Upon arrival, a Canadian border services officer will determine the traveler’s eligibility for entry and the length of their stay in Canada.

2. How do I verify an immigration agent?

To verify an immigration agent, you can check if they are registered with the appropriate regulatory body. In Canada, authorized immigration consultants are registered with the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants (CICC). You can visit the CICC website and use their "Find a Professional" tool to confirm the consultant's registration status.

3. Can I apply for multiple visas at the same time?

Yes, you can apply for multiple types of visas simultaneously, but it is essential to ensure that each application meets the specific eligibility criteria. It's best to consult with an immigration expert to determine the best approach based on your individual circumstances.

4. What happens if my application is refused?

If your application is refused, you will receive a letter explaining the reasons for the refusal. Depending on the reason, you may have options to appeal the decision, request a reconsideration, or submit a new application. We can guide you through the process of addressing any concerns or errors that may have led to the refusal.

5. Can I include my family in my immigration application?

Yes, most immigration programs allow you to include your immediate family members, such as your spouse or common-law partner and dependent children, in your application. Each family member must meet the eligibility criteria and provide the necessary documentation.
