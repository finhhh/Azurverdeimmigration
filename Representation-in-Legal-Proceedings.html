When Legal Representation Is Necessary

Legal proceedings may arise in various immigration contexts, including:

  • Appeals: Challenging a refusal of a visa, permit, or immigration application through appeals at various tribunals or courts.
  • Hearings: Attending immigration hearings, such as those related to admissibility, detention reviews, or refugee claims.
  • Judicial Reviews: Seeking a review of a decision made by an immigration officer or tribunal at the Federal Court of Canada.
  • Deportation and Removal Orders: Contesting removal orders or deportation proceedings initiated by immigration authorities.
