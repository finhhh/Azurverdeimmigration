Study. Work. Live in Quebec

Known for its rich culture, European influence, and strong French heritage, Quebec offers a unique Canadian experience. The province is renowned for its affordable, high-quality education system and diverse industries, making it an appealing choice for international students and professionals.

  • Full name: Province of Quebec
  • Population: 8.5 million (2021)
  • Capital: Quebec City
  • Largest city: Montreal
  • Area: 1.668 million sq km
  • Major language: French
  • Major religion: Christianity

Why Study in Quebec?

Quebec’s education system is accessible and affordable, with tuition fees lower than in many other regions. The province is home to several internationally acclaimed universities, and its focus on research and innovation makes it ideal for students seeking cutting-edge academic opportunities.

Big Cities:

Montreal, Quebec City, Laval, Gatineau, Sherbrooke

Climate:

Quebec experiences four distinct seasons, including snowy winters and warm summers. The climate supports a range of activities from skiing in winter to enjoying festivals in the summer.

Additional Information:

Quebec’s vibrant arts scene, diverse food culture, and strong bilingual environment make it a unique place to live. The province also offers pathways to permanent residence through the Quebec Experience Program (PEQ), which benefits students and workers who have spent time in the province.

Institutes:
  • McGill University
  • Université de Montréal
  • Concordia University
  • Université Laval
  • HEC Montréal
  • Université de Sherbrooke
Benefits:

Quebec offers a multicultural environment, affordable education, and specific pathways to permanent residency, making it ideal for international students and skilled workers.

  • Affordable tuition and living costs
  • Renowned universities and research centers
  • Permanent residency pathways like PEQ
  • Rich cultural experiences in a bilingual setting
